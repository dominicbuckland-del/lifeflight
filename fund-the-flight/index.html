What It Takes to Save a Life at 3,000 Feet

Every LifeFlight mission costs $4,375. Walk through where every dollar goes — then decide what it's worth.

Scroll to begin
Step 01

Crew Activation

The call comes in. A critical care doctor, flight paramedic, and rescue crew officer are paged. Within 60 seconds they're moving to the aircraft. Salaries, training, rostering — this is the cost of having the right people ready around the clock.

$380
Step 02

Fuel & Flight Ops

An AW139 helicopter burns roughly 300 litres of Jet A-1 per hour. Add navigation, comms, flight planning, and the ground crew that keep the aircraft turning. Most missions fly 200+ kilometres each way.

$1,240
Step 03

Medical Consumables

IV fluids, medications, intubation kits, surgical instruments, blood products, ventilator circuits. Every mission depletes supplies that must be restocked and quality-checked before the next call.

$890
Step 04

Medical Equipment

Portable ventilators, defibrillators, ultrasound machines, infusion pumps — ICU-grade devices hardened for flight. Every hour in the air is an hour of wear on equipment that costs hundreds of thousands to replace.

$640
Step 05

Base Operations

Helipads, hangars, crew quarters, communications infrastructure, 24/7 operations centres across Queensland. The lights stay on whether the phone rings or not.

$520
Step 06

Airframe Maintenance

Mandatory inspections, component replacements, airworthiness compliance. A helicopter requires roughly 12 hours of maintenance for every hour it flies. Safety is not optional — and it's not cheap.
