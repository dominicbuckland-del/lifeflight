01

The Call

Retrieval Services Queensland activates LifeFlight. A critical care doctor and aircrew are alerted within seconds. Every mission begins with one call that sets a chain of life-saving actions into motion.

Crew alerted in under 60 seconds
02

Wheels Up

The helicopter lifts off within minutes. On board: a doctor with ICU-level training, a critical care paramedic, and a rescue crew officer. They carry everything needed to treat the most critical patients.

Airborne within 8 minutes of activation
03

On Scene

The aircraft arrives. The doctor begins advanced interventions immediately — intubation, blood transfusions, surgical procedures — right where the patient fell. Hospital-grade care starts on the ground.

Average response time: 11 minutes
04

The Flying ICU

En route to hospital, the patient receives continuous ICU-level care at altitude. Ventilators, blood products, ultrasound, anaesthesia — everything a hospital ICU can do, in the air.

Full ICU capability at 3,000 feet
05

Handover

The patient arrives at a major trauma centre, stabilised. The LifeFlight crew hands over to the hospital team with a complete clinical record. Continuity of care, from paddock to emergency department.

Seamless transition to definitive care
06

The Outcome

8,000+ people helped each year. 90,000+ since 1979. Each one someone's mother, father, child, partner, friend. LifeFlight exists because every life is worth fighting for.

90,000+ patients since 1979
